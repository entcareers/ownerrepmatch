What Is an Owner's Representative?

An owner's representative acts as your advocate throughout a custom home or major renovation project. They help coordinate architects, contractors, consultants, budgets, schedules, contracts, and decisions — all while protecting your priorities.

For homeowners without the time, experience, or bandwidth to manage a complex build, the right representative provides oversight, accountability, and a single point of coordination so you can focus on the decisions that matter most.

How an Owner's Representative Can Help

Project Planning

Define goals, scope, priorities, timelines, and responsibilities before costly decisions are made or ground is broken.

Budget Oversight

Develop and monitor budgets, review costs, and help identify financial risks before they become larger problems.

Design Coordination

Coordinate architects, engineers, interior designers, and consultants throughout the design process.

Contractor Selection

Support requests for proposals, bid comparisons, interviews, negotiations, and contractor selection.

Construction Oversight

Monitor progress, schedules, costs, quality, communication, and project risks on your behalf.

Project Closeout

Coordinate final inspections, punch lists, documentation, warranties, handover, and move-in.
