What Is an Owner's Representative?

An owner's representative — sometimes called an owner's rep or construction advocate — is a professional who acts exclusively on your behalf during a custom home build, major renovation, or fire rebuild project.

When you're building or renovating a high-end home, you're coordinating architects, contractors, engineers, interior designers, and dozens of subcontractors. You're reviewing budgets, negotiating contracts, tracking schedules, and making hundreds of decisions — many with long-term consequences you may not fully understand in the moment.

An owner's representative brings experience, objectivity, and accountability to this process. They work for you — not the contractor, not the architect — and their job is to protect your interests at every stage.

What Does an Owner's Rep Do?

The specific scope varies by project, but owner's representatives typically help with:

  • Project Planning — Defining goals, scope, budget, and timeline before ground is broken
  • Team Selection — Vetting and selecting architects, contractors, and consultants
  • Contract Review — Ensuring contracts protect your interests and include appropriate terms
  • Budget Oversight — Tracking costs, reviewing change orders, and identifying financial risks
  • Schedule Management — Monitoring progress and holding teams accountable to deadlines
  • Quality Control — Reviewing work quality and ensuring specifications are met
  • Communication — Serving as your single point of contact across all parties
  • Problem Resolution — Addressing issues before they become costly disputes

When Do You Need an Owner's Rep?

Not every project requires an owner's representative. But you should strongly consider one if:

  • Your project budget exceeds $2-3 million
  • You don't have time to actively manage the project yourself
  • You lack experience with custom construction or major renovations
  • You're building remotely or can't be on-site regularly
  • You're rebuilding after a fire or disaster and navigating insurance claims
  • You want an independent professional reviewing contractor work and costs

Owner's Rep vs. General Contractor

A general contractor builds your home. An owner's rep makes sure it's built right — on budget, on schedule, and to your specifications. They're not adversaries; they work together. But the GC's business interests don't always align perfectly with yours. An owner's rep provides the independent oversight that keeps everyone accountable.

How Much Does an Owner's Rep Cost?

Fees typically range from 1-5% of construction costs, depending on project complexity and scope of services. For a $5 million build, that might be $50,000-$150,000. Most homeowners find this pays for itself through avoided mistakes, better contractor pricing, and fewer change orders.
